Report of Document Freedom Day 2015

Open Standards around the world

Document Freedom Day is the day when we talk about Open Standards around the world. In 2015, all together, we turned this day once more into a global event with 63 local event organisers in 31 countries on 4 continents. Volunteers around the world, accompanied by international organisations as well as politicians and public services joined our demand for document freedom. Read our report to see what happened during Document Freedom Day 2015.

Thanks to the motivation of local groups and amazing volunteers around the world, Document Freedom Day 2015 seems to be the largest ever, including 63 events in 31 countries. And although Europe and Latin America remain the epicenter of local activities, we are happy to see DFD is spreading more and more into North America with 4 events this year. Also, for the first time ever, Document Freedom was celebrated in Cuba, whose organizer says that "we took the first step in a long march towards our ultimate technological independence".

We have been very pleased to see that our call for Document Freedom made a big wave throughout France this year. After a rest period in 2014, there have been nine event registrations in France this year. This is not just an amazing increase, it even sets France as the most active country in 2015. Congratulations! This rise in events was accompanied by DFD promotions from our friendly organisations in France, among them Framablog, April, LinuxFr

Publications for Document Freedom Day

In the core of Document Freedom Day is and will ever be the local events throughout the world that spread the word about document freedom. And now, after celebrating the importance of Open Standards since many years now on a global scale, DFD is becoming established as the international day for Open Standards. This year, many international organisations took the chance of DFD to announce further steps towards a technological environment in that no one will ever be forced to use proprietary formats.

On Document Freedom Day April, European Digital Rights, Free Software Foundation Europe, Open Forum Europe and the Open Source Business Alliance were publishing a joint statement towards the European Commission to maximise "inclusiveness and engagement through the use of Open Standards", saying:

"With this joint statement, we call on the European Commission to address this situation and ensure that all interactions with the public can be performed entirely using Open Standards, thereby ensuring maximum inclusiveness and freedom of choice for all European citizens."

Also read quotes from affected users, that have been collected and published by Fixmydocuments.eu to support this joint statement.

Open Forum Europe used Document Freedom Day to release a toolkit that explains the principles and benefits of ODF, technical tools and highlights from the current UK Open Standards policy. For now, the toolkit contains infographics as well as a document titled "Open Document Format principles for Government Technology" which is intended to target faster public sector adoption of Open Document Format.

Icewarp and Collabora were using Document Freedom Day to announce their joint venture to produce an online LibreOffice version that "will become the free alternative to proprietary solutions [...], and the first to natively support the Open Document Format (ODF) standard". Although there is no date fixed yet, the plan is to release the first version by the end of the year.

Remix

For 2015, we have been promoting the use of our document freedom robots besides the DFD dove and we put them on our artwork page for everyone to reuse and remix. While we were very excited to see our robots used widely for various occasions, we hope for even more remixes the next time.

Another creative work was done by Sam Tuke from Collabora who was adding a DFD2015 Firefox plug-in that you can use as a skin for Firefox as well as for LibreOffice.

Political impact and outreach

The more our community achieves to spread the word about Document Freedom, the more it is recognized by politicians and public services around the world. In Germany, many political parties used Document Freedom Day to promote DFD and highlight the political importance of Open Standards, for example the Greens in Saxony, the Pirates in North Rhine-Westphalia, the Pirates in Brandenburg, and the Pirates in Heilbronn who were publishing an information portal HNwatch.de for every citizen to see current and former decisions by the municipality. We also have seen DFD events that have been organized or co-organized by public entities. On European level this were the Greens/EFA inside the European Parliament and in Argentina the Management and Modernization of Electronic Government.

Other political and public entities were promoting Document Freedom Day or specific local DFD events, for example the Free Software Commitee in Brazil, the local government of the Umbrian Region, the Ministry of Universitarian Education, Science and Technology from Venezuela, as well as a public website for teachers in Italy.

Cartoons and Illustrations

In 2015, a cartoon that we actually produced for 2014 circulated through different media. This produced a lot of attention towards the importance of Open Standards as well as the Document Freedom campaign. It also inspired local friends to translate the comic into their native language, even into Gallego. Now, the comic is available in 8 languages: Catalan, German, English, Spanish, French, Gallego, Russian and Turkish
